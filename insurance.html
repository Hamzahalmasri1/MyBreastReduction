Insurance Can Help Make Your Breast Reduction Dream a Reality

You Can Trust

Breast reduction surgery is considered a cosmetic procedure, and as such, is typically not covered by most medical insurance plans. However, in some cases, insurance may cover the cost of the procedure if it is deemed medically necessary.

Insurance may cover breast reduction surgery if the following conditions are met:

  • Medical Necessity

The procedure is performed to relieve physical discomfort and pain caused by large, heavy breasts. This may be documented by a primary care physician, specialist, or physical therapist.

  • Body Mass Index (BMI)

The patient's BMI is within the normal range, indicating that the large breasts are not due to obesity.

  • Documentation

The patient provides documentation of physical symptoms, such as neck and back pain, shoulder indentations, and skin irritation caused by large breasts.

  • Pre-Authorization

The insurance company has approved coverage for the procedure in advance, known as pre-authorization.

  • In-Network Provider

The patient uses an in-network provider for the procedure, which is covered by the insurance policy.

Insurance FAQ's

Is breast reduction surgery covered by insurance?

Breast reduction surgery is sometimes covered by insurance if it is performed for medical reasons, such as to relieve physical discomfort and pain caused by large, heavy breasts. The specific insurance coverage for the procedure will depend on the individual's policy and the circumstances of the case.

How do I know if my insurance covers breast reduction surgery?

To determine if your insurance covers breast reduction surgery, you will need to review your policy or contact your insurance provider. They will be able to provide you with specific information about your coverage and any costs you may be responsible for.

What documentation do I need to provide to prove medical necessity for breast reduction surgery?

To prove medical necessity for breast reduction surgery, you may need to provide documentation from your primary care physician or a specialist, such as an orthopedic doctor or physical therapist, confirming that your large, heavy breasts are causing physical discomfort and pain. You may also need to provide photos and measurements of your breasts.

How much of the cost of breast reduction surgery will my insurance cover?

The amount of the cost of breast reduction surgery that your insurance will cover will depend on your specific policy and the circumstances of your case. Some insurance providers will cover the entire cost of the procedure, while others may only cover a portion of the cost.

Will I need to pay a deductible or copay for breast reduction surgery?

Whether you will need to pay a deductible or copay for breast reduction surgery will depend on your specific insurance policy. Some insurance policies require a deductible or copay for certain types of procedures, while others do not.

Can I appeal if my insurance company denies coverage for my breast reduction surgery?

If your insurance company denies coverage for your breast reduction surgery, you may be able to appeal the decision. This will involve submitting additional documentation or information to support your case, and in some cases, a hearing may be scheduled.

How long does the insurance approval process take for breast reduction surgery?

The insurance approval process for breast reduction surgery can take several weeks or more, depending on the specific insurance company and the circumstances of the case. It is important to start the process as soon as possible to ensure that you have the coverage you need in a timely manner.
